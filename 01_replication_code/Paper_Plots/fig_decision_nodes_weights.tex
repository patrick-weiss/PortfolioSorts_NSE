			\footnotesize
			For each of the 68 sorting variables, we consider the paths of 14 decision forks for portfolio sorts until the premium is estimated. The first seven forks concern the sample construction: Excluding small stocks dependent on market equity quantiles (none, smaller than p(10) or p(20)), excluding financials (yes or no), excluding utilities (yes or no), excluding firm-months with negative book equity (yes or no), excluding firm-months with negative earnings (yes or no), stock-age exclusion (less than two years or none), and stock price exclusion (smaller than \$1, \$5, or none). The ensuing seven forks belong to the portfolio construction: The lag of the sorting variables (one month, three months, six months, or a \cite{fa/fr/1992} lag), the portfolio rebalancing (monthly or annually), the number of main portfolios (5 or 10), the sorting method (single sorts, independent or dependent double sorts), the number of secondary portfolios for double sorts (2 or 5), the exchanges for breakpoints (NYSE or all), and the weighting scheme (equal- or value-weighting). The color saturation indicates how often 109 papers analyzed by \cite{ho/xu/zh/2020} implemented each choice. \\[-8px]}
